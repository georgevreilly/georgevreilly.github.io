Review: The Heart's Invisible Furies

Title: The Heart’s Invisible Furies
Author: John Boyne
Rating: ★ ★ ★ ★ ½
Publisher: PUBLISHER
Copyright: YEAR
ISBN: 152476079X
Pages: 592
Keywords: fiction, gay, irish
Reading period: 30 October, 2018

[Spoilers below]

Cyril’s 16-year-old mother put him up for adoption in 1945, months after she was forced out of her Cork village by the parish priest. He grows up in an odd, well-off household in Dublin, always being told by his adoptive father, Charles Avery, that he’s not a real Avery. At seven, he develops a fas­ci­na­tion with Julian, the son of his father’s lawyer, whom he meets one fateful day. They meet again at fourteen, sharing a room in boarding school, and Cyril develops a full-blown but entirely secret obsession with Julian.

Cyril begins to realize that he is that most despised creature, a homosexual in 1950s Catholic Ireland. By twenty-one, he is frequently meeting other men for hurried, furtive, anonymous sex. Homosexual acts are illegal in Ireland and discovery would mean ruination, if not pros­e­cu­tion. He’s still secretly in love with Julian, now a het­ero­sex­u­al hedonist in the swinging Sixties. At twenty-eight, deeply conflicted, he marries Julian’s sister Alice, and abandons her hours after the wedding. By thirty-five (1980), he’s ended up in Amsterdam, where gay people can live openly; he starts a re­la­tion­ship with Bastiaan, a Dutch doctor. In 1987, they’re in New York, working with dying AIDS patients. One of them is Julian, from whom he belatedly learns that Alice has a teenaged son, Liam. Bastiaan is killed by fagbashers who leave Cyril with a limp.

After that, Cyril returns to Dublin, where he lives openly if quietly as a gay man. In 2015, Ireland has recently voted to legalize same-sex marriage, and Cyril, now seventy, marvels at his own 17-year-old grandson openly expressing affection for his boyfriend. It’s a bit­ter­sweet moment for Cyril.
