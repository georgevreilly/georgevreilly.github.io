Review: The Pawn

Title: The Pawn
Author: Steven James
Rating: $stars(4)
Publisher: Onyx
Copyright: 2009
ISBN: 0451412796
Pages: 448
Keywords: thriller
Reading period: 13–19 September, 2010

Patrick Bowers PhD is an en­vi­ron­men­tal crim­i­nol­o­gist for the FBI. A serial killer who calls himself the Il­lu­sion­ist is killing women in North Carolina and leading the FBI a merry dance. There's a major subplot involving another killer and Jonestown. And Bowers' personal life is com­pli­cat­ed by a sullen teenage step­daugh­ter.

Despite the ludicrous complexity of the plot, it's an en­ter­tain­ing and relatively thoughtful thriller.

Review: 1812: The Rivers of War

Title: 1812: The Rivers of War
Author: Eric Flint
Rating: $stars(4)
Publisher: Del Rey
Copyright: 2005
ISBN: 978-0345465689
Pages: 560
Keywords: alternate history
Reading period: 25 August–8 September, 2010

1812: The Rivers of War is the first novel in an alternate history series that recasts the War of 1812 and later the Trail of Tears. Sam Houston, Andrew Jackson, and several Scots-Irish and Indian characters head off in a somewhat different direction than they did in our history.

Flint tells a rousing, en­ter­tain­ing story, with exciting battles and engaging characters. It's a period of U.S. history that I knew little about, and I enjoyed the book.
